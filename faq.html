No. PLA is not biocompatible for permanent implantation. The printed piece is used only as a mold or shaping template — the actual implant is cast from a biocompatible material such as PMMA, then sterilized before surgery.
Because PLA has a low glass transition temperature, it cannot withstand standard steam autoclaving. Low-temperature methods such as hydrogen peroxide gas plasma sterilization (e.g. STERRAD™) are used instead, and a sterile barrier drape prevents direct PMMA contact with the mold.
Published studies report costs ranging from roughly $40 to $150 per mold when produced in-house on a desktop FDM printer — dramatically lower than outsourced titanium or PEEK implants, which can exceed $10,000.
