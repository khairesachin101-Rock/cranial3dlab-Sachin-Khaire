✅ Completed · Fronto-Parietal
Case 01 — Fronto-Parietal Defect with Lattice Implant
A right-sided fronto-parietal cranial defect, secondary to decompressive craniectomy, was reconstructed using the mirror technique in Meshmixer. The defect boundary was traced and mirrored from the healthy contralateral hemisphere across the sagittal plane, then offset into a Voronoi lattice structure to reduce implant weight while preserving structural integrity. Four titanium mini-plate fixation points were incorporated along the plate periphery for secure intraoperative anchoring. The final geometry was validated for wall thickness and manifold integrity before STL export for mold fabrication.

✅ Completed · Fronto-Temporal
Case 02 — Fronto-Temporal Lattice Plate (Lateral View)
This case documents a lateral-view design pass on a fronto-temporal lattice prosthesis, focused on refining fit against the curved temporal contours near the zygomatic arch. Screw-hole placement was repositioned closer to the fixation flange to avoid thin bone margins identified during the CT review. A 3–5 mm uniform offset was applied between the plate's inner surface and the native skull to prevent dural compression, and wall thickness was re-verified above the 3 mm clinical minimum prior to finalizing the mold geometry for casting.

✅ Completed · Parieto-Occipital
Case 03 — Large Parieto-Occipital Solid Plate
A large parieto-occipital defect exceeding 100 cm² required a solid (non-lattice) implant surface to provide adequate structural coverage over the affected region. The mirrored geometry was smoothed using Laplacian and Refine brushes to eliminate stepping artifacts from the CT reconstruction, ensuring a clean margin blend against the surrounding native bone. Given the defect's size and posterior load-bearing considerations, the design team opted against lattice perforation in favor of a continuous solid shell, prioritizing mechanical stability over weight reduction for this specific anatomical region.
