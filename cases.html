✅ Completed · Fronto-Parietal
Case 01 — Fronto-Parietal Defect with Lattice Implant
A localized fronto-parietal cranial defect was identified on the left side of the skull model. Using the mirror technique in Meshmixer, the defect boundary was traced and a corresponding patch was mirrored from the healthy contralateral side across the skull's midline. This mirrored patch was aligned and refined to match the surrounding bone curvature, then converted into a Voronoi lattice pattern to reduce the overall weight of the implant while maintaining an open cell structure. Small mounting tabs with fixation holes were added along the plate's edge for securing it in place. The completed lattice plate was test-fitted back onto the defect site on the skull model to confirm proper positioning before STL export.

✅ Completed · Fronto-Temporal
Case 02 — Fronto-Temporal Lattice Plate (Lateral View)
A small cranial defect was identified near the top of the skull model, toward the fronto-temporal region. The defect boundary was traced and mirrored from the healthy side using Meshmixer's symmetry tools, then converted into a hexagonal lattice pattern to reduce the plate's overall weight. The completed lattice plate was fitted back onto the skull to verify alignment and coverage over the defect area before finalizing the design.
📐 Defect: Fronto-Temporal 🪞 Mirrored anatomy 📏 3–5 mm offset 📅 2026

✅ Completed · Parieto-Occipital
Case 03 — Large Parieto-Occipital Solid Plate
This case involved a large posterior cranial defect spanning the parieto-occipital region, visible as an open cavity in the skull model. Given the size of the defect, a solid (non-lattice) plate was designed instead of a perforated lattice structure, to provide continuous surface coverage. The mirrored and smoothed plate surface was fitted over the defect on the skull model, and the plate geometry was also isolated separately to check its shape and thickness before export.
