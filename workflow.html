🗺️ The Pipeline

Five Stages, One Continuous Build

Every cranioplasty mold follows the same backbone — only the defect geometry changes.

Stage 01 · Imaging
CT Acquisition & DICOM Export
A 128-slice CT scan (≤1.25mm thickness) is taken pre-operatively. Raw DICOM series are exported from the PACS for segmentation.
DICOM
1
2
Stage 02 · Segmentation
Skull Reconstruction in 3D Slicer
DICOM data is thresholded (bone HU ≥ 250) inside 3D Slicer's Segment Editor, isolating the cranium from soft tissue and exporting a clean STL.
3D Slicer® · STL Export
Stage 03 · Modeling
Defect Mirroring & Plate Design
In Meshmixer, the defect boundary is selected, mirrored across the sagittal symmetry plane, thickened by 3–5mm, and Boolean-subtracted to form the implant plate.
Meshmixer® · Mirror + Boolean
3
4
Stage 04 · Mold Generation
Negative Mold & Slicing
A negative mold replicating the plate's outer surface is generated, split into two pieces, sliced in Cura with 0.2mm layers, and queued for FDM printing in PLA.
Cura® · FDM · PLA
Stage 05 · Surgical Use
Sterilization & Intraoperative Molding
Printed molds are sterilized (STERRAD™ or autoclave), draped, and used intraoperatively to shape PMMA bone cement into the final patient-specific implant.
STERRAD™ · PMMA Casting

🔬 Closer Look

Inside the Two Software Stages

Step A

Outline the Defect

With the "Select" brush and Symmetry enabled, the defect boundary is traced once — Meshmixer mirrors it automatically onto the intact side of the skull, giving an anatomically accurate target shape in seconds.

Boundary Accuracy98%
Step B

Mirror, Thicken, Subtract

The mirrored patch is separated, extruded to a 3–5mm prosthesis thickness, offset 1mm from the native bone to avoid a tight fit, then Boolean-subtracted to produce the final implant geometry — ready for mold generation.

Fit Tolerance±1mm
Step C

Mold & Print

The plate is shelled into a two-part negative mold. Each half is exported as STL, sliced in Cura at 0.2mm layers with 20–30% infill, and printed in white PLA — biocompatible enough to act as a sterile intraoperative former.

Avg. Print Time4–8 hrs
